The big question on everyone's mind is: who will replace Prime Minister Dr Keith Rowley?

Day one of the People's National Movement (PNM) Parliamentary Tobago retreat which began at 9 a.m. is currently under tight wraps, with secret talks being held at a private villa in Tobago Plantations, Lowlands, Tobago.

The two-day retreat is scheduled to take place today and tomorrow.

The names of some leadership contenders being mentioned include Energy Minister Stuart Young, Minister of Youth Development Foster Cummings, Foreign Affairs Minister Dr Amery Browne, Planning Minister Penelope Beckles, and Local Government Minister Faris Al-Rawi.

Several PNM parliamentarians are staying a short distance away at the Magdalena Grand Golf and Beach Resort. As they exited the hotel to attend the retreat, they remained tight-lipped about who will be chosen as the new leader.

Some took the opportunity to praise Rowley and express gratitude to him for giving them the chance to serve.

Tobago MPs thank PM

Prime Minister Dr Keith Rowley yesterday received praise and an outpouring of gratitude from the two Members of Parliament for Tobago he introduced to political life.

Tobago West MP Shamfa Cudjoe-Lewis and Tobago East MP Ayanna Webster-Roy credited Rowley for his leadership and vision in bringing young people into politics, and both expressed respect for his decision to retire from electoral politics.
